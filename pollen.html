What is Pollen?

Pollen is a fine powder produced by trees, flowers, grasses, and weeds to fertilize other plants of the same species.

Common symptoms of pollen allergies include sneezing, runny or stuffy nose, itchy or watery eyes, and throat irritation.

What is Mold?

Mold is a type of fungus that grows in moist environments. It can be found both indoors and outdoors.

Symptoms of mold allergies may include nasal congestion, throat irritation, coughing, wheezing, skin irritation, and eye irritation.

Allergy Management

To manage pollen and mold allergies, it's important to:

  • Avoid going outside during high pollen counts, particularly on windy days.
  • Use air purifiers and dehumidifiers indoors to reduce mold spores.
  • Keep windows closed during peak pollen season and use air conditioning instead.
  • Regularly clean and vacuum your home to remove pollen and mold.
  • Consider allergy medications or immunotherapy (allergy shots) for long-term relief.
